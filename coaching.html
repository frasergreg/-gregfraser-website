Common Questions

How is coaching different from advising, therapy, or talking to a peer or friend?

Unlike advising or consulting, where an expert tells you what to do, coaching partners with you to unlock your own wisdom and potential. I ask powerful questions, challenge your assumptions, and hold you accountable to your goals. I may occasionally offer strategic perspective from my 20+ years as a product executive, but the insights and decisions remain yours.

Unlike therapy, which focuses on healing past wounds, coaching is future-focused and action-oriented. Coaching helps you achieve specific professional goals and evolve as a leader.

As one Head of Product told me: "Greg changed my view of coaching. He doesn't try to be your path to nirvana—he helps you be a stronger leader and stay sane at the same time."

Unlike talking to peers, colleagues or friends, I bring neutral expertise without agenda or judgment. I'm not competing with you, don't need anything from you, and have no stake in your company politics. This creates a rare space for complete honesty and clarity.

How long does coaching take?

Most clients work with me for 6 to 12+ months. Real transformation takes time. You're building new neural pathways, shifting ingrained habits, and evolving how you show up as a leader.

That said, many clients notice meaningful shifts within the first few sessions. The work compounds over time.

Why do companies sponsor executive coaching?

Executive coaching delivers measurable ROI through:

  • Better decisions, faster - Fewer revision loops, strategic missteps, and avoidable mistakes.
  • Stronger teams - Higher retention and engagement. Leaders who are coached build teams that take ownership and innovate.
  • Reduced friction - Faster cross-functional alignment, less executive team conflict, and leaders who collaborate instead of escalate.
  • Executive retention - Preventing turnover that disrupts teams and costs 6-12 months of productivity.
  • CEO leverage - Less time spent coaching or correcting executives who operate more independently.

Most clients have their coaching sponsored by their company as a professional development investment, similar to executive education programs. I can help make the case to your manager, HR, or CEO. Some leaders self-fund.

What happens in a typical coaching session?

You bring the agenda with whatever is most pressing for you. We might dive deep into a specific challenge you're facing, work through a difficult decision, prepare for a crucial conversation, or explore ongoing patterns in your leadership that aren't serving you.

I'll ask powerful questions, challenge your thinking when appropriate, perhaps offer perspective from my experience, and help you land on clear next steps. You'll leave with both insights and action items.

Is everything confidential?

Absolutely. Everything we discuss is completely confidential. Even if your company is paying for coaching, I won't share the content of our conversations without your explicit permission.

The only exception would be if there were concerns about imminent harm to yourself or others, but that's exceptionally rare in executive coaching.
