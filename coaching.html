How It Works

We typically meet every other week (or weekly if preferred), at a consistent day and time. All sessions are remote via video. In our first few sessions, we'll establish your goals and create context for our work together.

6-12+ Months

Real transformation takes time. Most clients work with me for 6 to 12+ months to build new habits, shift mindsets, and create lasting change. That said, many clients notice meaningful shifts within the first few sessions.

Between-Session Support

I'm available asynchronously via email or text between sessions when you need a quick perspective or sounding board.

Confidential Partnership

Everything we discuss is completely confidential. This is your safe space to explore challenges, fears, and aspirations without judgment.

Common Questions

How is coaching different from advising, therapy, or talking to a peer or friend?

Unlike advising or consulting, where an expert tells you what to do, coaching partners with you to unlock your own wisdom and potential.

Unlike therapy, which focuses on healing past wounds, coaching is future-focused and action-oriented.

As a Head of Product told me: "Greg changed my view of coaching. He doesn't try to be your path to nirvana—he helps you be a stronger leader and stay sane at the same time."

Unlike talking to industry peers, colleagues or friends, I bring neutral expertise without agenda or judgment. This creates a rare space for complete honesty and clarity. As a CPO told me: "You can't get this kind of neutral, skilled support from anyone else in your life."

How long does coaching take?

Most clients work with me for 6 to 12+ months. Real transformation takes time. You're building new neural pathways, shifting ingrained habits, and evolving how you show up as a leader.

That said, many clients notice meaningful shifts within the first few sessions. The work compounds over time.

Why do companies sponsor executive coaching?

Executive coaching delivers measurable ROI through:

  • Better decisions, faster - Fewer revision loops, strategic missteps, and avoidable mistakes.
  • Stronger teams - Higher retention and engagement. Leaders who are coached build teams that take ownership and innovate.
  • Reduced friction - Faster cross-functional alignment, less executive team conflict, and leaders who collaborate instead of escalate.
  • Executive retention - Preventing turnover that disrupts teams and costs 6-12 months of productivity.
  • CEO leverage - Less time spent coaching or correcting executives who operate more independently.

Most clients have their coaching sponsored by their company as a professional development investment, similar to executive education programs. I can help make the case to your manager, HR, or CEO. Some leaders self-fund.
